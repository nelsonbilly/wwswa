Lost at Sea: First 24 Hours

Category: Disaster-Specific • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Maximize survival odds after a small‑boat capsize or engine failure until pickup.

Immediate Actions (HEAT)

  1. Hypothermia control: get as much body out of water as possible (raft/debris). Huddle if in group.
  2. Exposure management: use PFD hood/foil; avoid evaporative chill in wind.
  3. Alert: activate EPIRB/PLB, VHF Ch 16, mirror/whistle.
  4. Targets: stay near last known position; deploy bright marker.

Signaling Windows

  • Dawn/dusk: mirror is most effective; day: flags/panels; night: strobe/chem light rhythm.
  • Aircraft: signal when overhead pass starts—lead the pilot.

Example

Outboard dies 3 nm offshore. You anchor to avoid drift, call Pan‑Pan on VHF, and deploy a hi‑vis tarp on the bow. Sunstroke risk → rig shade with boathook + PFD.

Common Errors

  • Swimming for shore (exhausts, increases distance).
  • Drinking seawater.
  • Discarding bright gear.

Drill

Practice mirror aiming: two people 100 m apart; score hits by flashes. Learn strobe/morse SOS cadence.
