Navigating a City Without GPS

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Move through a city efficiently with dead phones by using grid patterns, transit corridors, and skyline features.

Grid Logic

  • Identify cardinal streets/avenues; odd/even numbering hints direction.
  • Follow transit lines (bus rails) to hubs with resources.

Landmarks

  • Use skyline features (towers, bridges) as big handrails.

Paper Map Method

  1. Mark start and destination; sketch two alternates.
  2. Note bridges/tunnels and barricade‑prone areas.
  3. Set rendezvous points every 15–20 blocks.

Real Example

Two coworkers walked home using a river as a handrail and a stadium as an attack point, avoiding a blocked downtown core.

Checklist

  • Pocket paper map
  • Sharpie, small compass
  • Cash for rideshare perimeter

Contingencies

  • Night travel → stick to lit arteries; avoid underpasses.
  • Unrest → move parallel to main routes; use alleys cautiously.

After‑Action

Update your pocket map with reliable cut‑throughs and safe lobbies.
