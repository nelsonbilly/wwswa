Pandemic-Style Hygiene & Masks in Disasters

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Reduce transmission during disasters with masks, distance, and surface routines that actually work.

Masks

  • N95/KN95 for crowded shelters or smoky air.
  • Fit check: nose bridge sealed; no gaps at cheeks.

Hands & Surfaces

  • Hand wash on arrival, before food, after bathroom/trash.
  • High‑touch surfaces: wipe daily with appropriate cleaner.

Airflow

  • Ventilate rooms; use HEPA or DIY filters if power allows.

Real Example

A family in a shared shelter rotated masks, used a simple hygiene schedule, and avoided respiratory illness over a two‑week stay.

Checklist

  • N95/KN95 masks
  • Soap, sanitizer
  • Disinfectant wipes

Contingencies

  • No masks → distance + ventilation become primary defenses.

After-Action

Rebuild a hygiene kit with dated masks and travel‑size soaps.
