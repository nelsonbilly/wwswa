Night Navigation by Stars & Moon

Category: Wilderness Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Reach a known feature without GPS using bearings, pacing, and terrain.

Scenario (Example)

Example: Dense conifer forest; limited visibility; intermittent ridgelines; two reliable backstops.

Step-by-Step

  1. Orient map; mark start, handrails, backstops.
  2. Shoot bearing; set pace count; confirm terrain features.
  3. Dogleg around obstacles; keep a running log.
  4. If lost: stop, think, observe, plan; move to known feature.

Real Example

Pace count every 100 m; confirmed at creek bend backstop; corrected 5° for crosswind drift.

Checklist

  • People: count, injuries, capability, morale.
  • Environment: terrain, weather, hazards, daylight.
  • Resources: water, calories, heat, shelter, comms.
  • Redundancy: Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency.

Contingencies

  • Primary fails twice → switch methods entirely.
  • Weather shift → shelter first, then resume.
  • Injury → stabilize, shorten moves, signal.

After-Action

Record what worked, what failed, and the first improvement to implement. Update your kit and this guide within 48 hours.
