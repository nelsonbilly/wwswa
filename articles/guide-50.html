Food Storage 101: Shelf Life, Rotation, Calorie Math

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Stock food that you eat, track shelf life, and hit real calorie targets without waste.

Targets

  • 2,000–2,400 kcal/person/day baseline; adjust for size/activity.
  • Mix of carbs/fats/protein for morale and nutrition.

Rotation (FIFO)

  • First In, First Out: label purchase/open dates; consume oldest first.

Category Picks

  • Grains: rice, oats, pasta
  • Protein: beans, lentils, canned fish
  • Fats: oils, nut butters
  • Flavor: spices, salt, broth

Menu Planning

Build 7–10 dinner templates and repeat; store what matches them.

Real Example

Household rotated a 30‑day pantry by cooking two “pantry dinners” weekly, replacing items monthly.

Checklist

  • Labels/marker
  • Bins by category
  • Spreadsheet or notebook tracker

Contingencies

  • No cooking fuel → choose no‑cook backups (nut butters, bars, canned fish).

After-Action

Review what you actually ate during outages and tune the pantry mix.
