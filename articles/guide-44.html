Sanitation When the Water Stops (Urban)

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Prevent illness during outages by handling waste, washing, and cleaning safely with limited water.

Toilet Options

  • Two‑bag system: Liner bag + absorbent + tie; second bag sealed for disposal.
  • Kitty litter or sawdust to control odor and moisture.
  • Bucket toilet: Seat on a 5‑gal bucket; bleach solution to sanitize.

Hand Hygiene

  • Soap + small water cascade beats sanitizer for grime.
  • Set a hand‑wash station with a spigot jug and catch basin.

Surface Safety

  • Disinfect kitchen/bath high‑touch areas daily.
  • Keep food prep separate from sanitation zones.

Real Example

Apartment used a bucket toilet with double bags and a dedicated bleach spray; no GI illness over a 5‑day outage.

Checklist

  • 5‑gal buckets + liners
  • Bleach and spray bottles
  • Soap, paper towels
  • Gloves

Contingencies

  • No bleach → soap/water clean + alcohol (≥60%) for touchpoints.

After-Action

Restock liners and document daily water use per person for future planning.
