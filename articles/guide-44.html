Surviving a Heatwave in an Apartment

Category: Disaster-Specific • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Execute a field-proven plan in the Disaster-Specific domain while protecting people, time, and options.

Scenario (Example)

Example: 48°C basin, 10 km traverse, one marked spring, zero shade at noon.

Step-by-Step

  1. Hydrate 0.5–1.0 L/hour + electrolytes.
  2. Pace 25/10 work-rest; longest rest at solar noon.
  3. Pitch shade low; reflective side out if available.
  4. Cool via wet bandana at pulse points; lighten load.

Real Example

Two-person team executed the plan in 22 minutes with zero injuries and conserved one liter of water.

Checklist

  • People: count, injuries, capability, morale.
  • Environment: terrain, weather, hazards, daylight.
  • Resources: water, calories, heat, shelter, comms.
  • Redundancy: Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency.

Contingencies

  • Heat exhaustion → stop/shade/electrolytes.
  • Heat stroke → aggressive cooling + evacuate.

After-Action

Record what worked, what failed, and the first improvement to implement. Update your kit and this guide within 48 hours.
