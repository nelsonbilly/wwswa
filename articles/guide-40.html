Staying Informed Without Internet or TV

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Maintain reliable information flow when usual channels are down.

Tools

  • Battery/hand‑crank AM/FM radio
  • NOAA/Weather band receiver (where available)
  • Community boards and local bulletin points

Intel Cycle

  1. Listen on the hour; log key updates.
  2. Check physical notice boards at set times.
  3. Share essentials with neighbors via a printed note.

Signal Discipline

  • Separate rumor from official notices; note sources and times.
  • Use one family member as the “intel scribe.”

Real Example

During a regional outage, a block captain posted twice‑daily summaries from radio briefings, reducing panic and duplicate rumors.

Checklist

  • Radio + spare batteries
  • Notepad, markers, tape
  • Map with marked shelters/clinics

Contingencies

  • No batteries → rotate between households with power banks.
  • Conflicting info → default to official emergency services channels.

After‑Action

Create a neighborhood “info loop” with posted times and responsible volunteers.
