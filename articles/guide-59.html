Water Disinfection: Boil, Chemicals, Filter, UV

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Choose the right water treatment for your situation by understanding what each method does and doesn’t do.

Methods

  • Boil: Rolling boil 1 minute (3 min at high elevation); kills pathogens; needs fuel.
  • Chemicals: Chlorine/iodine tablets; effective for bacteria/viruses; wait times 30–60 min; taste affects morale.
  • Filters: 0.1–0.2 μm for protozoa/bacteria; many don’t remove viruses; clog in silty water; pre‑filter helps.
  • UV: Fast and effective in clear water; batteries required; won’t work in turbid water.

Combos

Filter + chemical covers most threats, with better taste than chemicals alone.

Real Example

Team filtered river water then used UV for speed at camp; switched to chemical overnight for extra margin.

Checklist

  • Primary and backup method
  • Pre‑filter (bandana/coffee filter)
  • Treatment dosing card

Contingencies

  • Very silty → settle water or use alum/flocculant if available.

After‑Action

Record doses and wait times that worked; stage spare parts for filters.
