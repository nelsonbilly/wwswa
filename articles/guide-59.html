How to Survive a Zombie Apocalypse

Category: General • Last updated: 2025-08-10

Objective

Employ proven survival principles in a fictional (we hope) zombie outbreak scenario — focusing on mobility, resource security, and group cohesion — while having a little fun with the end of the world.

Scenario (Example)

Example: A fast-moving infection spreads via bites, contact, or air (depending on the movie you believe). Cities are in chaos within 72 hours. The undead are slow but relentless. Resources are scarce, and human threats are often worse than zombies.

Checklist

  • People: Who’s in your crew? Skills, fitness, trust level.
  • Environment: Urban choke points, rural escape routes, safe zones.
  • Resources: Food (non-perishable), water, weapons/tools, fuel.
  • Comms: Radios, agreed-upon signals, rally points.
  • Morale: Entertainment, routine, and a reason to keep going.

Protocol

  1. Initial 24 Hours: Avoid crowds, secure a defensible location, inventory supplies.
  2. 72-Hour Mark: Relocate to a safer zone before infrastructure collapses entirely.
  3. Daily Rhythm: Patrol, forage, maintain gear, train, and rest — in that order.
  4. Contact Rules: Treat all strangers with caution; the infected aren’t the only threat.

Contingencies

  • Breach: Have an escape route from every location.
  • Injury: Know how to treat bites and wounds — or when to say goodbye.
  • Supply Loss: Cache gear in multiple locations to avoid total wipeout.
  • Group Split: Pre-plan rally points and timeframes for regrouping.

Pro Tips

  • Silent is survival: Loud noises = dinner bell for the undead.
  • Don’t overpack: You need speed more than comfort.
  • Stay unpredictable: Change routes and routines to avoid ambushes — zombie or human.
  • Think like a prepper, move like a scout: This is a marathon, not a sprint.

After-Action

Review each close call: What attracted them? What could you have done faster or quieter? Update your plan nightly. Zombies don’t adapt — but humans do.
