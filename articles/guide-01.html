Top 10 Items for Your First Bug-Out Bag

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Assemble a 72‑hour kit that you can actually carry, maintain, and deploy under stress. This is a build you can complete in an afternoon with common gear—then refine.

Baseline Scenario (Example)

Example: 2 adults evacuating a city after a prolonged blackout. You’ll walk 8–10 miles/day to stay with family. Water sources are uncertain, cell network is unreliable.

Step‑by‑Step Build

  1. Pack selection (20–30L daypack). Try it on with 20 lb. If seams creak, swap. Frame or stiff back panel helps. Example: 26L pack + hip belt keeps weight off shoulders.
  2. Water (Priority #1). Two 1L bottles + 1L collapsible bag + squeeze filter (0.1 μm). Example: 2×1L Smartwater bottles in side pockets; CNOC bag rolled inside.
  3. Calories. Target 2,000–2,500 kcal/day × 3 days. Mix bars, nuts, jerky, tuna packs. Example: 12 bars (250 kcal each) + 2 bags trail mix + 4 tuna pouches.
  4. Shelter & heat. 5×7 ft tarp, emergency bivy, compact poncho, lighter + ferro rod, 6–8 stakes, 50 ft paracord. Example: A‑frame tarp + bivy inside keeps wind off.
  5. Medical. Tourniquet, pressure bandage, gauze, tape, meds (ibuprofen, loperamide, antihistamine), blister kit. Example: Small zip case on top for fast access.
  6. Light. Headlamp with lockout + spare batteries. Example: 200–350 lm headlamp; keep in outer pocket to find things at night.
  7. Navigation. Paper map, small baseplate compass, printed route card. Example: Pre‑mark water points and bridges.
  8. Tools. Small fixed blade or sturdy folder, multitool, duct tape flat‑pack, safety pins. Example: Wrap 6 ft of tape around a card.
  9. Comms & admin. Whistle, Sharpie, notepad, cash ($40–$80 small bills), spare phone cable + power bank (10,000 mAh). Example: Note rally points and times.
  10. Clothing. Wool socks, base layer, beanie, gloves. Example: Vacuum bag a spare pair of socks—they’re morale and blister control.

Packing Order (from bottom to top)

  1. Soft/light: spare clothing, sleeping layer.
  2. Dense: food, water, power bank (centered near your back).
  3. Fast‑access: medical, headlamp, snacks, rain gear, map, whistle.

Weigh & Walk

Target 15–20 lb before water. Walk one mile. Adjust straps, move painful items, and trim redundancies.

Common Mistakes

  • Overpacking cookware; underpacking water treatment.
  • No blister kit → trip ends early.
  • Heaviest items far from back → sore shoulders, wasted energy.

10‑Minute Drill

Lay everything out. Start a timer. Pack in the order above and be out the door within 10 minutes. Repeat monthly.
