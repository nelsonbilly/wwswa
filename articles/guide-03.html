Fire-Making: 5 Methods Without Matches

Category: Wilderness Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Create sustained flame in wind and rain with multiple ignition methods.

Scenario (Example)

Example: Two-person team, mixed terrain, limited daylight, one minor injury, low batteries.

Step-by-Step

  1. Stage fuel ladder: tinder → kindling → thumb-thick fuel.
  2. Ferro rod from above; pull rod back to keep tinder stable.
  3. Split wet wood to expose dry cores; teepee or log-cabin build.
  4. Shield from wind; feed gradually to sustain.

Real Example

Two-person team executed the plan in 22 minutes with zero injuries and conserved one liter of water.

Checklist

  • People: count, injuries, capability, morale.
  • Environment: terrain, weather, hazards, daylight.
  • Resources: water, calories, heat, shelter, comms.
  • Redundancy: Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency.

Contingencies

  • Primary fails twice → switch methods entirely.
  • Weather shift → shelter first, then resume.
  • Injury → stabilize, shorten moves, signal.

After-Action

Record what worked, what failed, and the first improvement to implement. Update your kit and this guide within 48 hours.
