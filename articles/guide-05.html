Surviving a Blackout: Home Checklist

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Stabilize safety, light, food, and communication at home for a multi‑day outage.

Scenario (Example)

Example: Citywide outage at 6:30 PM. Elevators down, cellular spotty. You live on the 8th floor.

First 15 Minutes

  1. Check for hazards (gas smell, broken glass, stuck elevators). If gas odor → evacuate and call utility from outside.
  2. Shut off/unplug electronics; leave one light switched on for power‑return indicator.
  3. Grab headlamp + radio + phone power bank; text family status.

First Hour

  1. Water: Fill bathtub and pitchers now; later the pumps may stop.
  2. Food: Move perishable items you’ll eat soon to a cooler; keep fridge closed.
  3. Light: One lantern per room; place away from curtains. No open flames if you can avoid it.
  4. Stair Plan: If you must leave, carry a small kit; avoid dark stairwells alone.

First Night

  • Keep a flashlight at bedside; secure doors and windows.
  • Set a 2‑hour device charging schedule to conserve power bank.
  • Track updates via FM radio or cell if up.

Day Two

  • Cook from fridge/freezer first (portable stove outside or by open window with detector).
  • Rotate batteries; check on neighbors, especially elderly.
  • Top up water if the system is still running.

Apartment Example

8th‑floor resident uses a cooler, solar power bank by the window, and a door wedge. Water stored in collapsible containers; stairwell recon during daylight.

Common Errors

  • Burning candles near drapes.
  • Opening fridge “just to check.”
  • No ventilation when cooking indoors.

10‑Minute Drill

Kill your main breaker (or simulate). Stage one lantern per room, fill containers, and list what you’re missing.
