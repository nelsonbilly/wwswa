Home Medical Kit: Beyond Band-Aids

Category: General • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Stop bleeding, maintain airway, treat shock, evacuate early.

Scenario (Example)

Example: Two-person team, mixed terrain, limited daylight, one minor injury, low batteries.

Step-by-Step

  1. MARCH: Massive bleeding → Airway → Respiration → Circulation → Head/Hypothermia.
  2. Tourniquet high & tight; note time; pressure bandage for venous bleeds.
  3. Recovery position if breathing; observe chest rise.
  4. Evacuate early on red flags.

Real Example

Tourniquet high & tight stopped arterial bleed in 20 seconds; time marked; evac initiated.

Checklist

  • People: count, injuries, capability, morale.
  • Environment: terrain, weather, hazards, daylight.
  • Resources: water, calories, heat, shelter, comms.
  • Redundancy: Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency.

Contingencies

  • Uncontrolled bleed → second tourniquet proximal.
  • Airway compromise → recovery position; evac.

After-Action

Record what worked, what failed, and the first improvement to implement. Update your kit and this guide within 48 hours.
