Best Survival Knives Under $100

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Select a knife that survives prying, carving, food prep, and light batoning without breaking the bank.

Specs That Matter

  • Blade: 3.5–5 in., 2.5–4 mm thick, drop/clip point.
  • Steel: 1095, 8Cr13MoV, or Sandvik 12C27—easy to sharpen in field.
  • Tang: Full or robust partial; avoid hollow handles.
  • Handle: Grippy when wet; no hotspots.
  • Sheath: Solid retention, drain hole, multiple carry options.

Field Test Protocol

  1. Feather sticks: curls without tearing.
  2. Notch: clean 90° notch in thumb-thick stick.
  3. Split: baton wrist-thick wood—no edge roll.
  4. Food: slice an apple/tomato thinly (control test).

Care

  • Dry and oil carbon steel; strop often.
  • Sheath clean to avoid grit wear.

Common Errors

  • Oversized blades reduce control.
  • Gimmick saw-backs.
  • No lanyard—easy to lose at night.

5-Minute Drill

Carve a pot-hanger notch and a feather stick; time yourself. Compare knives and pick the one that controls best for you.
