Best Survival Knives Under $100

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Select a knife that survives prying, carving, food prep, and light batoning without breaking the bank.

Key Specs (What Matters)

  • Blade: 3.5–5 in., 2.5–4 mm thick, drop or clip point.
  • Steel: 1095, 8Cr13MoV, Sandvik 12C27 — easy to sharpen in field.
  • Tang: Full or robust partial for strength.
  • Handle: Grippy when wet (rubberized or textured G10).
  • Sheath: Retentive, drain hole, multiple carry options.

Test Protocol (Example)

  1. Feather sticks: curls without tearing.
  2. Notch carving: clean V‑notch in a thumb‑thick stick.
  3. Baton split: wrist‑thick green wood—no edge rolling.
  4. Food prep: slice an apple/tomato thinly.

Buying Tips

  • Prioritize ergonomics over marketing steel names.
  • Skip saw‑back gimmicks and hollow handles.
  • Budget for a small field sharpener.

Care

  • Dry and oil carbon steel; strop after each trip.
  • Sheath with blade clean to avoid grit wear.

Common Errors

  • Oversized blades reduce control.
  • Chisel grinds that wedge in wood.
  • No lanyard—easier to lose at night.

5‑Minute Drill

Carve a pot hanger notch and feather stick; time your performance and compare between knives.
