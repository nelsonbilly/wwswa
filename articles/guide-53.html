Tarp Shelter Mastery: Six Fast Pitches

Category: Wilderness Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Pitch the right tarp for wind, rain, and terrain in under 5–8 minutes.

Six Pitches

  1. Flat roof: Calm weather shade; high and airy.
  2. A‑frame: General purpose; ridge line between trees.
  3. Lean‑to: Wind from one direction; reflect fire heat.
  4. Plow point: One corner high, three staked; fast storm option.
  5. Flying V: Two corners high; good for cooking in light rain.
  6. Storm pyramid: Center pole; edges to ground; excellent for heavy weather.

Hardware

  • 8–10 stakes, 2 poles or sticks, 15–20 m of cord, two prusiks for ridgeline tension.

Real Example

Team switched from A‑frame to plow point as wind shifted; setup time dropped to 4 minutes with practice.

Checklist

  • Pre‑tied guy lines
  • Ridgeline with quick‑attach prusiks
  • Groundsheet

Contingencies

  • No trees → center‑pole pyramid using trekking poles.
  • Rocky soil → deadman anchors with buried sticks/rocks.

After‑Action

Practice each pitch twice in good weather; time yourself.
