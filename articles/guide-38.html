Safe Indoor Lighting Solutions During Blackouts

Category: Urban Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Provide safe, effective lighting without causing fires or CO hazards during outages.

Light Types

  • LED lanterns (diffuse, room‑wide)
  • Headlamps (task lighting)
  • String lights on power banks (low draw)

Placement

  • One lantern per room at shoulder height; avoid placing near curtains.
  • Night path lights: dim markers from bedroom to bathroom/kitchen.

Battery Strategy

  • Assign devices to specific battery sizes to simplify spares.
  • Rotate recharge schedule with solar panel or car adapter by day.

Do Nots

  • No candles near fabric; never leave flames unattended.
  • No fuel lanterns indoors without ventilation and CO alarm.

Real Example

Apartment used a lantern-per-room setup and low-output path lights to prevent trips, cutting battery swaps to once daily.

Checklist

  • LED lanterns/headlamps
  • Rechargeables + charger
  • CO alarm (battery)

Contingencies

  • No recharge option → ration to essential rooms only.

After‑Action

Standardize lights on two battery types and stock accordingly.
