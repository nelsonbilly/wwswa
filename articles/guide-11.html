Cold Weather Survival Basics

Category: Wilderness Survival • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Layer, vent, and insulate to avoid hypothermia and frostbite.

Scenario (Example)

Example: −15°C windchill, spindrift, dusk approaching; damp base layer.

Step-by-Step

  1. Layer: wicking base, insulating mid, wind shell.
  2. Vent on climbs; change damp base early.
  3. Insulate from ground; hot drink before sleep.
  4. Warm gradually; avoid rapid reheating of frostbitten tissue.

Real Example

Dry base layer swap at camp prevented night chill; hot drink + insulated pad stabilized core temp.

Checklist

  • People: count, injuries, capability, morale.
  • Environment: terrain, weather, hazards, daylight.
  • Resources: water, calories, heat, shelter, comms.
  • Redundancy: Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency.

Contingencies

  • Frostbite → rewarm gradually, protect from refreeze.
  • Hypothermia → shelter, dry layers, warm fluids.

After-Action

Record what worked, what failed, and the first improvement to implement. Update your kit and this guide within 48 hours.
