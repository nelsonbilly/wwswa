DIY Perimeter Alerts (Ethical & Safe)

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-10

Objective

Set simple, non‑injurious alerts so you wake up or get notified when someone enters a boundary.

Principles

  • Detect, don’t injure. No traps. Local laws apply.
  • Visibility: alerts should be obvious to you, discreet to passersby.

Methods

  • Bell-on-line: light cord at ankle height to jingle a bell.
  • Clipped glow stick: trip line pops a glow stick visible from your tent.
  • Battery driveway sensor: off‑the‑shelf, fast setup.

Placement

  • At camp edges, near gear caches, and at stairwells or back gates.

Real Example

Backyard storage alerted by a driveway sensor and a simple bell‑line deterred repeated night rummaging without confrontation.

Checklist

  • Bell or light noisemaker
  • Light cordage
  • Driveway sensors

Contingencies

  • Pets present → place lines higher; avoid entanglement.

After‑Action

Adjust line height and sensitivity after a night of testing.
