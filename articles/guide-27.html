Avoiding Dangerous Wildlife Encounters

Category: Wilderness Survival • Last updated 2025-08-09

Mission

Avoiding Dangerous Wildlife Encounters: run a clear plan with minimal friction. Wilderness Survival context dictates tempo, but fundamentals stay the same—protect people, time, and options.

Risk Picture

  • Environment shifts faster than your plan.
  • Equipment fails or is missing.
  • Human factors: fatigue, cold/heat, stress.

Protocol

  1. Assess: hazards, exits, resources, weather.
  2. Decide: pick the simplest plan with margin.
  3. Execute: two–five minute cycles; reassess.
  4. Communicate: short, confirmed messages.

Loadout

  • Primary tool for the task
  • Alternate method (different failure mode)
  • Consumables: water, fuel, batteries
  • Admin: IDs, maps, notes, cash

Execution Notes

Slow is smooth; smooth is fast. If a step fails twice, change methods, not effort. Keep logs: time, actions, and resource levels.

Contingencies

  • Primary → Alternate → Contingency → Emergency
  • Injury → slow tempo, shelter, signal
  • Nightfall/weather → cover and heat

Training

Short monthly reps beat rare marathons. Drill the critical path until it’s boring, then add stress and time limits.
