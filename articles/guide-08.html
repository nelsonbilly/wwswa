Solar Chargers for Emergency Power

Category: Preparedness & Gear • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Keep phones, radios, and lights alive during outages using a portable solar + power bank setup you can trust.

System Basics

  • Panel 10–28W (folding), USB‑A/C outputs.
  • Battery 10,000–20,000 mAh with pass‑through charging.
  • Cables: short, high‑quality (USB‑C/Lightning/Micro‑USB).

Setup (Example)

  1. Place panel facing sun at ~30–45°; avoid shade bands.
  2. Panel → power bank by short cable; bank → device(s).
  3. Use phone’s battery saver; disable screen‑hungry apps.

Throughput Reality

  • 10W panel in real sun → ~5–6Wh/hour. Expect ~30–40% of the label.
  • Cloudy? Charge the bank all day; top devices at night.

Mounting & Safety

  • Clip to pack only when stopped; hiking angle is poor.
  • Keep bank shaded; heat kills batteries.

Common Errors

  • Long cables causing voltage drop.
  • No pass‑through bank → devices cut out with clouds.
  • Panel partly shaded—output collapses.

Field Drill

Run a 10W panel for 4 hours into a 10,000 mAh bank; record % gained. Adjust angle hourly and compare.
