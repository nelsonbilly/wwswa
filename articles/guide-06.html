Earthquake Safety Before, During, and After

Category: Disaster-Specific • Last updated 2025-08-09

Objective

Reduce injury and damage from earthquakes with pre‑secure measures and correct reactions during and after the event.

Before (Home Hardening)

  • Bolt bookcases and water heater; latch cabinets.
  • Store heavy items low; secure TV/monitors with straps.
  • Know gas shutoff location; keep an adjustable wrench nearby.

During (Drop, Cover, Hold On)

  1. Drop to hands and knees—stay put.
  2. Cover under sturdy furniture or next to interior wall.
  3. Hold on to shelter until shaking stops.

After (First 30 Minutes)

  1. Check for injuries; stop bleeding first.
  2. Smell or hear gas? Turn off gas outside and evacuate. Don’t use switches or phones inside.
  3. Avoid elevators; expect aftershocks; move to open area.

Apartment Example

Bookshelves strapped, TV tethered, cabinet latches installed. Family practices reaching safe spots in each room within 3 seconds.

Neighborhood Check

  • Help neighbors with heavy furniture hazards.
  • Listen to local radio for damage and shelter info.

Common Errors

  • Doorways are not structurally safest—use sturdy furniture.
  • Running outside during shaking.
  • Lighting matches after a strong quake.

Drill

Set a 60‑second timer: each family member moves to a safe spot in room A, then B. Repeat monthly.
